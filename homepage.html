Welcome to the Flexible Timetable Registration

Empowering Your Academic Journey at Amity University

Amity University proudly offers flexible timetable options to its students, empowering you to design your academic schedule according to your individual needs and aspirations. This innovative system allows you to select courses that align with your interests and manage your time effectively, fostering a truly personalized and balanced educational experience.

Choice-based Credit System:
Amity University operates on a dynamic choice-based credit system, providing you with the autonomy to select courses and craft your academic planning worksheet, thereby gaining greater control over your educational path.
Slot-based Timetable:
Your course and time selections are made from a meticulously designed slot-based timetable, which is readily accessible and comprehensively published on Amizone, ensuring transparent and convenient planning.
Individual Timetables:
Leveraging this system, students can generate their own unique individual timetables by choosing from the available slots, guaranteeing a schedule that perfectly complements their commitments and learning style.
Flexibility in Balance:
The inherent flexibility of this system is a cornerstone of our approach, enabling students to effectively balance rigorous academic engagement with diverse personal interests, extracurricular activities, and other vital commitments, promoting holistic development and well-being.

Guidelines for Creating an Effective Timetable

To ensure a smooth and successful timetable registration process and to maximize your academic planning, please adhere to the following comprehensive guidelines:

  1. Course & Lab Slotting: Theory components of courses are strategically distributed across various designated slots (e.g., A1 to G2). Similarly, all practical/lab components are assigned to specific lab slots (e.g., L1 to L40). It's important to note that a single slot may contain multiple course offerings, and conversely, a single course might be offered across multiple different slots to provide maximum choice.
  2. Course Selection & Customization: You, as a student, possess the autonomy to decide precisely which courses you wish to register for. Once chosen, you can then select the corresponding slots that best fit your schedule. In scenarios where the same course within a single slot is delivered by multiple faculties in various classrooms, you are empowered to choose your preferred venue and faculty.
  3. Dedicated Assistance for Registration: Should you require any guidance or encounter challenges during the course registration process, dedicated support will be readily provided by your assigned Mentor or through our accessible Help Desk, conveniently located on the 2nd floor, Room No. 218, Computer Lab.
  4. Prioritization & Adaptability: It is crucial to meticulously plan your course selections and slot choices in conjunction with the pre-filled slot timetable. There may be instances where you need to exercise flexibility and make alternative choices (in course, slot, venue, or even faculty) to successfully register for a specific course you've prioritized.
  5. First-Come, First-Served Basis: All course registrations operate strictly on a first-come, first-served basis. To maintain fairness and ensure the integrity of the registration process, no proxy registrations are permitted under any circumstances. Students must register personally.
  6. Addressing Full Class Situations: In the event that a particular class has reached its maximum capacity, students still have the option to register for that course by exploring and selecting a different available slot or an alternative venue where space is available.
  7. Theory Embedded Lab (TEL) Courses - Complete Registration: For Theory Embedded Lab (TEL) courses, it is imperative to understand that registration is deemed complete only when both the theory component and all associated lab components are individually registered. If either the theory or any required lab component is not registered, the entire registration into that course will be considered incomplete. Practical Tip: To avoid issues, upon completing registration for the theory part of a TEL course, proceed immediately to complete the registration for its corresponding lab(s) before moving on to other courses.
  8. Re-registration of Failed Courses: Should you need to re-register for a previously failed course, you may do so if the same course is currently being offered. Confirmation of this re-registration is contingent upon your ability to register without any timetable clashes and is strictly subject to the timely payment of applicable re-registration fees.
  9. Credit Range Recommendations: Students are strongly advised to register into courses recommended by their respective Program School. The recommended credit range for a semester typically falls within a minimum of 16 credits and a maximum of 27 credits. This range is suggested to optimize academic workload and is subject to the individual student's academic ability and personal interests.
  10. Guidance for Open Elective Courses: For detailed information, guidance, and assistance related to registering for Open Elective courses, please do not hesitate to contact your Mentor, School Timetable Coordinator, Head of Department (HoD), or Head of Institution (HoI). They are available to provide comprehensive support.
  11. Final Timetable Confirmation: Upon the successful completion of the registration process for all your chosen courses (including both theory lectures and practical labs), this finalized schedule will officially serve as your definitive timetable for the current semester.
